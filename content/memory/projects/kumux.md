Kumux was a real challenge — I started my first days during COVID lockdown. I'd learned React just two weeks before joining, and had to level up fast while delivering production code at the same time. That sink-or-swim pressure accelerated my growth more than anything before it.

The core product was a frontend tool for lighting engineers: they could fine-tune illumination by adjusting dozens of colorimetric parameters, and the app would compute and visualise the optimal lighting output. I went deep into the lighting and colorimetry domain — spectral data, colour rendering indices, chromaticity — translating that into interfaces that were precise enough for engineers but still usable.

The main technical challenge was performance: slow renders with complex colorimetric datasets made the UI feel broken even when the data was correct. I worked on decoupling visual logic from data fetching and optimising the rendering pipeline to keep it responsive.
